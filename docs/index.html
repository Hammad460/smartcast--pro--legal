Privacy Policy

This policy explains how SmartCast Pro handles information when you use casting, remote control, screen mirroring, private vault, voice, subscription, notification, analytics, crash reporting, and remote configuration features.

Effective date July 15, 2026
Data sale We do not sell your personal information.
Tracking We do not use your data to track you across other companies' apps or websites.

This Privacy Policy applies to SmartCast Pro for iPhone and iPad, including its broadcast extension. The app is designed to help you discover nearby TVs and casting devices, control Android TV devices, cast media, mirror your screen, manage private local files, use voice input, receive app notifications, manage subscriptions through Apple, measure app usage, diagnose crashes, and remotely manage app settings and feature availability.

SmartCast Pro does not require you to create an account. Most app data is stored on your device and is used only to provide the app features you choose to use.

Information We Collect or Process

Depending on the features you use, SmartCast Pro may collect or process the following information:

  • Local network and device information: nearby Chromecast, Google Cast, Android TV, and compatible receiver names, device identifiers, model names, IP addresses, connection status, and pairing status. This is used to discover, connect to, and remember selected devices.
  • Photos, videos, and files you select: media chosen from your photo library, file picker, or private vault. Selected media may be temporarily served from your device over your local network so your TV can play it.
  • Private vault content: photos, videos, and files you intentionally import into the private vault. This content is stored locally on your device in the app's storage.
  • Screen mirroring content: screen video frames captured by Apple's ReplayKit broadcast extension while you actively mirror your screen.
  • Voice and speech data: microphone audio and speech recognition results when you use TV voice search or voice remote features.
  • Subscription information: product identifiers, purchase state, transaction status, and entitlement status handled through Apple StoreKit.
  • App preferences: onboarding status, theme choice, selected device records, premium access state, private vault passcode settings, security question settings, and similar local settings.
  • Notifications: if you allow notifications, the app may use notification permission status and device notification services to send subscription reminders, app reminders, or feature-related alerts.
  • Analytics events: app events such as app launches, screen views, feature usage, button taps, subscription flow events, casting actions, remote control actions, mirroring actions, notification interactions, and similar usage events. These events may include device and app information, approximate region, language, app version, operating system version, timestamps, and Firebase-generated identifiers such as an app instance identifier.
  • Firebase Remote Config information: Firebase installation identifiers and related app or device attributes used to fetch and apply app configuration values, feature availability, paywall content, experiments, and app behavior.
  • Crash and performance diagnostics: crash logs, stack traces, app state at the time of a crash, device model, operating system version, app version, crash timestamps, installation identifiers, and related diagnostic information used to understand and fix stability problems.
  • Technical information: connection diagnostics, WebRTC connection statistics, signaling room identifiers, and error messages used to operate casting and mirroring features.

How We Use Information

We use information only for app functionality, including to:

  • discover and connect to compatible TVs and casting devices on your local network;
  • send remote control commands, keyboard input, app launch commands, and voice search data to your selected TV;
  • cast selected photos, videos, and files to your TV;
  • mirror your screen to a receiver during an active mirroring session;
  • store and display private vault content locally inside the app;
  • process, verify, restore, and unlock subscription purchases through Apple;
  • send notifications you have allowed;
  • understand which app features are used and improve app usability through analytics events;
  • remotely manage app settings, feature availability, paywall content, experiments, and app behavior without requiring an app update;
  • detect, investigate, prioritize, and fix crashes, bugs, and performance issues;
  • save your app preferences and selected device information; and
  • diagnose connection problems and improve reliability.

Permissions

SmartCast Pro asks for access only when needed for a feature:

  • Local Network: used to find and communicate with TVs and casting devices on your Wi-Fi network.
  • Bonjour services: used for Google Cast and compatible receiver discovery.
  • Photo Library: used to browse, select, cast, import, or optionally delete photos and videos you choose.
  • Microphone: used for voice search and voice remote features.
  • Speech Recognition: used to transcribe voice input for TV search features. Speech recognition may be processed by Apple depending on your device settings and Apple's speech recognition service behavior.
  • Screen Recording / Broadcast: used only when you start screen mirroring through ReplayKit.
  • Notifications: used only if you allow notifications, such as subscription trial reminders or app-related alerts.

Local Storage

SmartCast Pro stores app preferences, selected device records, subscription entitlement flags, and private vault content on your device. Private vault content is not uploaded by SmartCast Pro to our servers as part of the vault feature. If you delete the app, data stored only inside the app may be deleted by iOS.

Casting and Local Network Streaming

When you cast selected media, SmartCast Pro may create a temporary local web server on your device so the selected TV can stream that media over your Wi-Fi network. This stream is intended for devices on your local network during the casting session. Do not use casting on networks you do not trust.

Screen Mirroring and WebRTC

When you start screen mirroring, the broadcast extension captures screen video frames and sends them to your selected receiver using WebRTC. SmartCast Pro may use a signaling server, STUN server, and TURN server to establish and maintain the connection. Signaling messages can include temporary room IDs, session descriptions, ICE candidates, IP-related network routing information, connection state, and diagnostic information.

Screen content is processed only while a mirroring session is active. You should stop mirroring before displaying sensitive information you do not want shown on your TV or processed through the mirroring connection.

Voice Features

When you use voice search or voice remote features, microphone audio may be captured, converted, sent to your selected TV for voice input, and sent to Apple's speech recognition service for transcription where applicable. SmartCast Pro does not use voice input for advertising or tracking.

Subscriptions and Payments

Purchases and subscriptions are processed by Apple through the App Store. SmartCast Pro receives purchase and entitlement information from Apple to unlock premium features, but we do not receive your full payment card number or Apple ID password. Subscription management, cancellation, refunds, and billing are handled by Apple.

Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, and Remote Config

SmartCast Pro may use Firebase Analytics to collect app usage events, Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash reports and diagnostic information, and Firebase Remote Config to remotely manage app settings, feature availability, paywall content, experiments, and app behavior. Firebase is provided by Google. Analytics helps us understand general app usage, feature engagement, and subscription flow performance. Crashlytics helps us identify crashes, group related issues, understand how many users are affected, and fix stability problems. Remote Config helps us return configuration values for your app instance without requiring an app update.

Firebase data may include automatically collected events, custom events, app instance identifiers, Firebase installation identifiers, device and app information, approximate region, crash stack traces, diagnostic logs, and app or device attributes used for Remote Config. If we use Remote Config conditions, Analytics audiences, A/B Testing, or personalization, Remote Config may work with Firebase Analytics data to deliver different configurations and measure results. We do not use Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, or Remote Config data to sell personal information. We do not intentionally send sensitive private vault content, photos, videos, files, microphone audio, speech transcripts, or screen mirroring video content to Firebase as analytics, crash reporting, or Remote Config data.

Google may process Firebase data according to Google's Firebase privacy and security terms. More information is available from Firebase's privacy documentation.

Third-Party Services and SDKs

SmartCast Pro uses third-party frameworks and services to provide app features, including Apple frameworks, Google Cast SDK, Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, Firebase Remote Config, WebRTC, GCDWebServer, Lottie, and MBProgressHUD. These services may process technical information necessary to provide their functionality. If a third-party service is used for casting, mirroring, speech recognition, payments, analytics, crash reporting, remote configuration, or device discovery, its handling of information may also be governed by that provider's privacy terms.

Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may share or transmit information only as needed to provide the features you use:

  • to your selected TV or casting receiver for casting, remote control, voice, or mirroring;
  • to Apple for StoreKit purchases, subscriptions, speech recognition, notifications, and operating system permissions;
  • to Google Cast-compatible services and devices for casting and receiver communication;
  • to Google Firebase for analytics events, crash reports, diagnostic information, and Remote Config values;
  • to WebRTC signaling, STUN, or TURN infrastructure when needed for screen mirroring; and
  • if required by law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.

Data Retention

Local preferences and private vault content remain on your device until you delete them, reset the app, or uninstall the app. Temporary casting files, mirroring session data, voice buffers, and connection diagnostics are intended to be used only for the active feature session or short-term troubleshooting. Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, and Remote Config data is retained according to Google's Firebase retention practices and your Firebase project settings. Apple may retain purchase, speech recognition, and notification information according to Apple's policies.

Your Choices

  • You can grant or revoke permissions in iOS Settings.
  • You can delete private vault items inside the app.
  • You can disconnect from casting devices and stop screen mirroring at any time.
  • You can manage or cancel subscriptions in your Apple ID subscription settings.
  • You can disable notifications in iOS Settings.
  • If the app provides analytics or crash reporting controls, you can use those controls to change Firebase data collection preferences where supported.
  • You can delete the app to remove app-local data stored by SmartCast Pro, subject to iOS backup and system behavior.

Children's Privacy

SmartCast Pro is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, contact us at smartcastprosupport@gmail.com so we can review the request.

Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect information handled by the app. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. You are responsible for using trusted Wi-Fi networks, protecting your device passcode, and stopping casting or mirroring when appropriate.

International Use

If you use SmartCast Pro outside your country, information needed for app features may be processed in countries where Apple, Google, WebRTC infrastructure providers, or our service infrastructure operate.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when app features, permissions, services, or legal requirements change. The updated version will be posted on this page with a new effective date.

Contact

For privacy questions, requests, or support, please contact us at smartcastprosupport@gmail.com.
